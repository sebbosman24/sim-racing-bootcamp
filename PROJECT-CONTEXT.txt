SIM RACING BOOTCAMP — PROJECT CONTEXT
======================================

ROLE & MOTIVATION
-----------------
Built by Sebastian Bosman, Senior Product Manager.
The goal: build a top-of-funnel growth lever for Track Titan — a sim racing coaching platform — by targeting the fast-growing F1 fan audience. F1's global fanbase has grown 63% since 2018, driven by a younger, Drive to Survive-origin cohort with deep emotional investment in the sport but no pathway into sim racing. This product bridges that gap and funnels qualified users directly into Track Titan.


RESEARCH PHASE
--------------
Before a single line of code was written, five AI research agents were spawned in parallel to validate the opportunity and define the product approach.

Agent 1 — F1 Growth Trajectory
Investigated: F1's global audience growth, demographic shifts, the Drive to Survive effect, viewership trends by region, and the emerging cohort of fans who identify with F1 as culture rather than just sport.

Key findings:
- F1 fanbase grew from 490M to 827M between 2018 and 2024 (+63%)
- The fastest-growing demographic: 16–35 year olds, with significant female audience growth
- Drive to Survive created a cohort of identity-driven fans — they follow the drama, the drivers, the circuits — and increasingly want to participate, not just watch
- iRacing hit 300,000 simultaneous active accounts in January 2025, suggesting real demand for sim racing
- No product exists to convert F1 fan interest into a first sim racing session

Agent 2 — F1 Fan to Sim Racing Funnel
Investigated: the actual conversion path from F1 fan to sim racer, where people get stuck, what decisions they face, and what motivates or blocks them.

Key findings:
- The five-layer bridging problem: fans hit consecutive barriers — hardware paralysis, game selection confusion, technique gaps, and sim ecosystem illiteracy — with no single product guiding them through
- Most YouTube content covers hardware and game selection but skips driving technique entirely and never explains the online racing ecosystem (safety ratings, incident points, flag rules)
- The emotional hook is identity, not instruction — fans want to feel like drivers, not students
- "Budget" and "platform" decisions create friction too early; identity questions build engagement

Agent 3 — Sim Racing Onboarding Landscape
Investigated: every major onboarding touchpoint in the sim racing ecosystem — iRacing, Assetto Corsa Competizione, Gran Turismo 7, YouTube channels (Boosted Media, Jimmy Broadbent, Aarava, Coach Dave Academy), Reddit communities, and existing beginner guides.

Key findings:
- iRacing is "functional for informed users, actively hostile to cold-start newcomers"
- ACC gives beginners zero onboarding: setup options appear immediately with no guidance
- Gran Turismo 7 is the best-in-class for onboarding but its physics don't transfer to simulation
- No YouTube channel has produced a sequenced, progressive beginner series — content is episodic and topic-based, not designed for learning
- The gap: no product bridges F1 fan → sim racing competence with a structured, personalised, interactive flow

Agent 4 — Track Titan Positioning
Investigated: Track Titan's current product, audience, and positioning relative to the sim racing coaching market.

Key findings:
- Track Titan is a telemetry coaching platform — primarily serving active sim racers who want to improve
- No pre-acquisition funnel exists; Track Titan has no content or product targeting the "I want to start sim racing" phase
- The product-market fit gap: Track Titan speaks to existing sim racers, not the F1 fan who hasn't yet made the leap
- An onboarding funnel that delivers identity-affirming personalisation could drive significant top-of-funnel volume

Agent 5 — Product Validation & Virality Mechanics
Investigated: what makes quiz-based identity products shareable, how to design for viral loops, and what signals from the F1 community indicate demand.

Key findings:
- Personality-based results that feel "uncannily accurate" drive social sharing — the identity label is the product
- F1 fans respond strongly to personality framing tied to real drivers (Verstappen as aggressive risk-taker, Hamilton as smooth strategist, Alonso as tactical mastermind)
- Shareable cards with a named archetype create the viral loop: "I'm The Monaco Specialist — what are you?"
- The quiz itself must feel like an F1 personality test, not a shopping decision tree


HOW RESEARCH SHAPED THE PRODUCT
---------------------------------
Every major product decision traces directly back to research findings:

1. The quiz was originally 7 questions including budget range and gaming platform — designed for hardware recommendations. Research showed these are purchasing decisions that create friction for cold paid-ad audiences. The quiz was redesigned around identity: what draws you to F1, which driver speaks to you, which circuit would you start on. No transactional questions.

2. The Driver DNA archetype — the named, F1-coded personality type — became the core of the product. Research confirmed that identity-affirming results are the single biggest driver of sharing behaviour. Everything else became secondary to getting this moment right.

3. Hardware recommendations were removed from the results page entirely. Research showed that presenting a £299 wheel recommendation to someone who just clicked a Facebook ad is premature and off-putting. The hardware conversation belongs inside Track Titan, not the funnel.

4. The 4-week curriculum was removed. Time-based commitments ("Week 1: do this, Week 2: do that") create pressure rather than confidence for someone who hasn't yet committed to the sport. It was replaced with four simple, concept-based steps — Your Rookie Season — framed as insights, not a schedule.

5. The share card was made a centrepiece. Research confirmed the archetype label is the viral mechanic. The DNA card lives inside the results page with one-tap sharing to X and Reddit. The named archetype is what gets screenshotted.

6. The circuit selection question was included specifically to personalise step one of the Rookie Season — "Learn your favourite track: Silverstone" — creating a moment of personalisation that feels immediate and specific.

7. The CTA was rewritten to connect Driver DNA to Track Titan's telemetry value rather than hardware readiness: "Your Driver DNA is just the start. Track Titan's telemetry will show you exactly how your natural style plays out on circuit — and where the tenths are hiding."


THE PRODUCT STRATEGY
---------------------
Sim Racing Bootcamp is not a sim racing onboarding tool. It is a growth lever — a paid ad funnel built specifically for F1 fans, designed to:

1. Make the user feel seen and identified (Driver DNA archetype)
2. Give them enough context to feel like an insider (Rookie Season steps)
3. Create a shareable identity moment (the card, the archetype name)
4. Hand them to Track Titan with confidence and momentum (the CTA)

The product is stateless — no accounts, no database, no friction. A user arrives from an ad, answers 7 questions, gets their Driver DNA in under 10 seconds, and is one tap from Track Titan.


HOW IT WORKS
------------
1. User lands from a paid ad targeting F1 fans
2. Takes a 7-question identity quiz (feels like an F1 personality test, not a form)
3. F1 start lights animation plays while Claude generates their profile (turns API latency into a branded moment)
4. Results reveal: Driver DNA archetype with name, 4 single-word traits, and share buttons
5. Your Rookie Season — 4 conceptual steps into sim racing, step 1 personalised with their chosen circuit
6. Track Titan CTA with personalised hook connecting their DNA to telemetry coaching
7. Share to X or Reddit — the archetype name drives organic loops


THE QUIZ (7 QUESTIONS)
-----------------------
All identity-based, no transactional questions:

1. How did you discover Formula 1? (Drive to Survive / Always been a fan / Friends & family / F1 games)
2. What draws you to F1? (Strategy / Raw speed / Rivalries & drama / The circuits)
3. Which driver's style speaks to you? (Verstappen / Hamilton / Leclerc / Alonso / Russell)
4. Have you tried sim racing before? (Never / F1 games on controller / Wheel setup / Sim race regularly)
5. How would you describe your approach? (Instinctive / Methodical / Aggressive / Patient)
6. What's the goal? (Master a circuit / Beat people online / Understand racing like a driver / Just feel it)
7. Which circuit would you start on? (Silverstone / Monaco / Spa / Monza / Suzuka / Interlagos / Singapore / Bahrain)

Each answer feeds a context string injected into the Claude prompt — 7 signals that shape the archetype, traits, tagline, and Track Titan hook.


THE DRIVER DNA
--------------
Generated by Claude Haiku from all 7 quiz signals. Output:
- Archetype name: evocative, F1-coded ("The Monaco Specialist", "The Apex Hunter", "The Data Whisperer")
- 4 single-word traits displayed as visual tiles with icons: ⚡ ◎ ▲ ◆
- Archetype tagline: one sentence that makes them feel uncannily seen
- Track Titan hook: personalised bridge from their DNA to Track Titan's coaching

The Driver DNA is the product. Everything else frames and amplifies it.


YOUR ROOKIE SEASON
------------------
Four hardcoded conceptual steps — not a time-based curriculum, not a schedule. Framed as the things every sim racer must internalise, in order:

1. Learn your favourite track: [circuit they chose] — personalised from question 7
2. Discover the racing line — the insight that works everywhere
3. Pick your braking points — consistency before bravery
4. Smooth is fast — throttle control, the car rewards patience

Designed for someone who hasn't committed yet. Concepts, not instructions. Confidence, not pressure.


TECH STACK
----------
- Framework: Next.js 16.1.7 (App Router, React 19, Turbopack)
- Styling: Tailwind CSS v4 (CSS-first config, custom brand tokens)
- Font: Racing Sans One (display) + Geist Sans (body)
- AI: Anthropic Claude Haiku via @anthropic-ai/sdk — server-side only, structured JSON output
- Encoding: base64url for shareable Driver DNA card URLs
- OG images: next/og ImageResponse for dynamic share card previews
- Rate limiting: in-memory IP-based (10 req/min)
- Deployment: Railway via nixpacks, standalone Next.js output
- State: sessionStorage for quiz answers — fully stateless, no database


DESIGN DECISIONS
----------------
Colour: #F67A22 (Track Titan orange) replaces the original F1 red. The brand signal is Track Titan, not Formula 1.

Loading state: F1 start lights animation — 5 lights illuminate one by one, then go out. Converts 5–8 seconds of API latency into a recognisable, branded moment that F1 fans immediately understand.

Results layout: single page, no overwhelming scroll. DNA card at top (archetype + traits + share), Rookie Season below, Track Titan CTA at the bottom. Designed to feel like a driver briefing card — complete at a glance.

Typography: Racing Sans One for archetype names — the display font does the emotional heavy lifting. Everything else is clean and minimal.

Share card: integrated into the DNA card itself. One-tap share to X or Reddit. The archetype name is the tweet. "I'm The Monaco Specialist — what are you?"


PRODUCT ITERATION LOG
---------------------
The product went through significant strategic iteration during build:

Version 1 — Sim Racing Onboarding Tool
Original framing: a structured onboarding flow for new sim racers. Featured hardware recommendations (wheel, pedals, 3-month upgrade), game recommendations, and a 4-week curriculum with 12 specific session descriptions. Aimed at someone who had decided to start sim racing.

Version 2 — Growth Hack Reframe
Reframed as a paid ad funnel for Track Titan targeting F1 fans. Key insight: the original audience (someone who has decided to buy a wheel) is much smaller than the target audience (F1 fan who is curious about what driving would feel like). The product needed to meet people much earlier in their intent journey.

Changes made in reframe:
- Quiz: removed budget, platform, weekly hours (transactional) → replaced with draws, approach, circuit (identity)
- Results: removed hardware recommendations entirely
- Results: removed 4-week curriculum → replaced with 4 conceptual Rookie Season steps
- Results: brought share card to the foreground as a primary feature
- CTA: rewritten from hardware-readiness to DNA-to-telemetry bridge
- Landing page: removed "exact hardware to buy" copy → "Discover your Driver DNA"

Version 3 — Visual Simplification
Multiple rounds of copy and design reduction based on the principle that overwhelm kills conversion:
- Removed trait pills from DNA card → replaced with icon + single word tiles in a row
- Removed hardware "why" descriptions → name and price only, then removed entirely
- Removed race prediction from DNA card → archetype name alone carries the moment
- Removed all section copy headings like "Sim Racing Bootcamp" and "Your Racing Plan"
- "Your Journey" → "Your Rookie Season" (F1-coded, positions user as a driver not a fan)
- Share card: redesigned from text pills to 4 visual icon tiles, integrated into DNA card


OUTCOME
-------
- A stateless, sub-10-second quiz-to-result experience designed for paid ad traffic
- Driver DNA archetype creates a shareable identity moment that drives organic loops
- The Rookie Season gives F1 fans enough context to feel confident without overwhelming them
- Track Titan CTA is the only conversion point — clean, singular, motivated
- Built to run on Railway with zero infrastructure overhead and no database dependencies
